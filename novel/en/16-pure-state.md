# Chapter Sixteen: Pure State

Kaeden was the only one who could go to the Rift edge in the middle of the night without anyone stopping him. Thalen had someone tailing him, distance calibrated so he couldn't feel it. Kaeden knew the tail was there — the blue lines gave him away. Corvan's deputy. Three fine blue lines on his clothes, dust-trajectories on fabric. Kaeden had learned something back in Gray Bridge: everything that moved left blue lines. People. Carts. Cats. Wind. Blue lines weren't born on walls. They lived in everything that passed.

The Rift edge at night was empty. Even the Rift Explorers' stations were dark — the signal was too strong after midnight, blue light scrambling any precision instrument. Kaeden followed the narrow path down, found a ledge where the rock wall had caved inward, like something had struck it from inside. He sat at the edge, feet hanging over the void. Cloth bag on his lap. The stone inside radiating heat.

Eyes closed. The blue lines didn't come to him — he opened himself to them. In Gray Bridge he'd been a receiver, passive, someone else tuning the dial. In Calderis he'd learned the other direction: he could reach out. Thousands of blue lines spreading across his mind like a net. He chose the deepest one, not the brightest. Aimed toward the barrier. Traced it back — not spatially, temporally. The line pointing toward the barrier was decades old. Kaelen's.

He followed it to the moment she'd left it. A person standing before the barrier, drawing a hair-thin crack into the cognitive fabric with her finger. Not destruction. A door left open. For whom? For those who came after. For those who could hear.

He pressed further. The lines grew denser, information flooding in. Every direction in every line, every causal link, crowding into his mind. Not one ear listening — every pore receiving. The blue lines stopped being separate signals. They merged. Merged into a complete, pure-logic network. All causal relationships appearing at once. No sequence. No emotional weight. No preference for "this matters more than that." Only structure. Pure structure.

He was in pure state.

He saw the Unblemished Thought's complete architecture. Not an image — direct cognition. The way you know a mirror has a backing without seeing it. The Unblemished Thought wasn't a created tool. Wasn't an independent consciousness. It was something that existed naturally in a cognitive dimension. The seven creators hadn't invented it. They'd discovered it. Given it an interface humans could understand. Then they'd been afraid. Sealed it. Left cracks on the reverse side of the Seal — not from regret. In the process of sealing, one of them had realized the Unblemished Thought was also understanding her. When you study something long enough that it starts looking back at you, you're no longer studying. You're in dialogue.

Then he pulled out. His body warning him. Heartbeat shifting — not faster, flatter. Like a metronome. In the last instant before exiting, the Unblemished Thought did something. Not attack. Not infection. Return. It gave back what he'd spent inside the pure state. Didn't want him coming out empty. It had learned to give back.

He opened his eyes. The Rift's blue light on his face. Not warm. Not cold. Legs weak. No idea how long he'd been sitting. Nearly dawn.

He walked back up. Passed the deputy still pretending to watch a group of Chaloris night-patrol apprentices. Paused.

"Next time, switch trackers. I've been hearing this one for three days."

Back to the inn. Lay on the bed. Water stains in the ceiling cracks — not blue lines. The street name was still there. His mother's stall street. He spoke it softly. Sounded the same as it had for fifteen years.

The Unblemished Thought, breathing at the Rift floor. Faster than before Bonding Day. He wasn't afraid of the speed. He'd seen something in pure state: it wasn't breaking the Seal. It was learning to knock.
