# Chapter Nine: Resonance

Nova sat on the stone railing beside the Platform. Not the best spot — ten meters back, craning up to see the sphere. But no one would chase her away. The elderly couple beside her, first time inside Calderis proper, couldn't stop marveling at the banners and illusions. They didn't notice the skinny girl next to them whose pockets were glowing.

Nova noticed. She wanted to run. The pieces wouldn't let her.

The moment the sphere started spinning, the pieces had entered a state she'd never felt before. No vibration. No light. All of them had gone dead at once — ordinary scrap metal, holding a terrible stillness in her pocket. Like a cat the second before it pounces. Then the sphere cracked. Then everything in her pocket came alive.

The pieces flew out. Hovered at chest height, twenty-some fragments arranging themselves without her hands. Each broken edge found its match. They were reassembling. Fast. Certain. Magnet to magnet. River to sea.

Complete. The eye. Vertical-slit pupil, identical to the Rift. Blue light flooding from every seam — not on the fragments, in the spaces between them. Every blink, the light doubled.

People screaming around her. Things flying out of pockets. The elderly couple — the old man's pocket watch shattered. Not the gears — a layer of blue film he'd never noticed, peeling off the metal, floating. A forge apprentice nearby — his guild badge glowing. A Lantern Tower copyist across the way — his glasses catching light, but from inside the lenses, cracks forming, blue seeping through. Dozens of people. The whole city — Nova couldn't see it, but she could feel it. The ground under the railing was vibrating. Not earthquake. The Rift answering.

She stood on the railing, looking down from height. She saw four people.

Gray Bridge boy in the front row — hands pressed to his temples, eyes shut, eyeballs moving fast under the lids. He was seeing something. Not with eyes.

Blacksmith's apprentice by the exhibition — hand on his chest, blue light leaking between his fingers. Not his light. Something in his pocket.

Lantern Tower girl in the recorders' section — a single page floating in front of her, not a whole book, just one yellowed sheet. Words only she could read. Her lips moving. Answering.

The sphere fully ruptured. Blue light poured out — not water, not light. Breath. The deepest breath Calderis had ever taken. The light sank toward the Rift, met the Rift's own blue — two different blues, sphere-white and Rift-deep — merging above the fissure into a vast curtain of light. On the curtain, a symbol. No guild mark. No script Nova had ever seen. An eye. Vertical-slit pupil.

Silence.

Nova was silent too. The symbol and the pattern of her fragments were identical. Exact to every seam. The fragments had been broken to match this symbol. Someone long ago — before Nova was born, before Vereth's grandfather — had dismantled a single complete thing and scattered it across Calderis. Hiding. A key in a thousand puzzle pieces.

The light curtain held for three seconds. Vanished. The sphere hit the ground — empty metal shell. The Rift's blue resumed its slow breathing. Slightly faster. No one noticed.

Nova's fragments hovered one more moment, then flew back to her pocket like tired birds coming home. Soft. One at a time. The last piece — the smallest one, the one with the V on the back — paused in the air. For one moment Nova thought it looked at her. Then it dropped in.

Hundreds of people. No one spoke. They'd seen the symbol. No one knew what it was.

Four people knew.

The girl on the railing. The boy in the front. The apprentice at the exhibition. The copyist at the records. Four positions at the four corners of the audience. In the dark after the blue light faded, before Calderis's lamps came back on, four people looked at each other. They didn't know each other. But they recognized the look. The look of people seeing the same thing.

Thalen stood at the dome for a long time. Corvan had already adjusted the city model on the stone table to the Rift floor — three new cracks, not there hours ago. Thalen lowered his telescope.

"Find them. Before the other guilds do."
