# Chapter Fourteen: The Warehouse

The third time Nova found Lynx was in a stairwell behind the Weavers' Hall. Not because she had a lead — because she'd figured out Lynx never appeared in the same place twice. Last time, the bridge. Before that, the black-market alley. Now: the Weavers' back stairwell, a place even stray cats avoided. Weavers' threads drifted down from the windows and got caught in cat tails. Cats hated it. Nova wasn't fond of it either. But she knew he'd be here.

Lynx sat on the middle step, exactly at the boundary between light and shadow. His notebook open on his knee. When Nova approached, he didn't close it. Not trust — his notebook was only readable to him. Symbols. Abbreviations. Arrows. Circles. All encrypted in a language whose key existed only in his head. Nova had seen Lantern Tower scholars' notes, black-market ledgers, inter-guild cipher correspondence. Lynx's notebook was a fifth thing. Not a codebook. A map. A map of people. She saw herself — not a name, a circle. Two lines from her circle: one to a box labeled "blue-light fragments," one to an empty circle with a question mark inside.

"Who's that." She pointed at the empty circle.

Lynx glanced at it. "Don't know. But his circle is the same size as yours."

"What does circle size mean."

"Not importance. Unpredictability."

Nova set coins on the step. Lynx didn't take them. She doubled the amount. Still didn't take them. Not haggling — the next piece of information was worth too much to sell cheap.

"Rift Explorers' outer warehouse. Number forty-seven."

"You gave me that address already."

"Last time I gave you the address. This time I'm giving you—" He closed the notebook. "Last time I didn't know what was inside. Now I do. The things in there weren't forgotten. They've been guarded. Over a hundred years." He stood, passed her, headed out. Paused at the doorway. "Vereth. The warehouse keeper. Go find him."

Nova waited in the back alley until dark. The Rift Explorers' outer warehouses were a row of gray low buildings along the Foundry's waste channel. Forty-six: spare parts. Forty-eight: decommissioned equipment. Forty-seven: no windows. One rusted iron door. No number on the door. A tiny plaque on the hinge: 47.

The guard was an old man. Rift Explorers uniform, with a name stitched beneath the guild crest. Vereth. He was the keeper.

Inside, the warehouse was stacked by era. Boxes layered on boxes. The bottom layer had rotted — touch them and they crumbled, revealing blue-light fragments wrapped in oiled paper. Same material as Nova's street scraps, but more complete. Each piece had its own number and label. The labels didn't show sale prices. They showed years. The oldest — she checked the bottom crate — a date carved into the wood. Over a hundred years ago.

She worked her way up. Newer boxes, newer labels. Fifty years. Thirty. Ten. Five. Every crate held fragments glowing faint blue — sleeping, docile, like animals in cages. Not waiting to die. Waiting for someone.

On the top crate, a work log. Vereth's private notebook. Handwritten. Each line: a date, then a minimal description. Nova flipped to the last entry. Dated a few days ago.

"Crate 47. 12 new pieces. Source unknown. Delivered by: Lynx."

Nova closed the log.

Lynx wasn't an information broker. He was making choices. Delivering fragments where they belonged. Vereth was the receiver, the end of a hundred-year lineage. Lynx was the distributor. Taking pieces from a source and giving them to the guardian.

She left the warehouse in full dark. Took nothing. Just pulled the smallest piece — the one with the V on the back — from her pocket and placed it beside the log. Not for Vereth. For Lynx. To let him know someone had found the first one.
